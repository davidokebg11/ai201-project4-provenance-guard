# Provenance Guard — Planning Document

## Architecture Narrative

A piece of text enters the system via POST /submit. It carries a `text` field and a `creator_id`. The system passes the text through two independent detection signals: (1) an LLM-based classifier via Groq that reads the text holistically and returns a probability it is AI-generated, and (2) a stylometric heuristics analyzer that computes statistical properties of the text (sentence length variance, type-token ratio, punctuation density) and returns a score based on how "uniform" the writing is. Both signal scores are fed into a confidence scorer that combines them into a single float between 0 and 1. That score is mapped to a transparency label. The result — including content_id, attribution, confidence, label, and both signal scores — is written to the audit log and returned to the caller.

An appeal enters via POST /appeal with a `content_id` and `creator_reasoning`. The system looks up the original classification, updates its status to "under_review", appends the appeal to the audit log, and returns a confirmation.

## Detection Signals

### Signal 1: Groq LLM Classifier
- **What it measures:** Semantic and stylistic coherence holistically — does this text "read" like AI output? Captures things like overly balanced sentence structure, corporate-speak, hedging phrases, and unnaturally smooth transitions.
- **Output:** A float between 0 and 1 (0 = confident human, 1 = confident AI)
- **Blind spot:** Cannot detect AI text that has been heavily edited by a human, or human text that is naturally formal and structured (e.g., academic writing).

### Signal 2: Stylometric Heuristics
- **What it measures:** Statistical surface properties — sentence length variance (AI text tends to be more uniform), type-token ratio (vocabulary diversity; AI repeats words more), and punctuation density.
- **Output:** A float between 0 and 1 (0 = very variable/human-like, 1 = very uniform/AI-like)
- **Blind spot:** Short texts give unreliable statistics. Also, some humans write very consistently (e.g., technical writers), which may score as AI-like.

## Uncertainty Representation

- **Score 0.0 – 0.39:** Likely human. Confidence is high enough to lean toward human attribution.
- **Score 0.40 – 0.64:** Uncertain. Neither signal is confident enough to make a strong call.
- **Score 0.65 – 1.0:** Likely AI. Both signals agree the text has AI-like properties.

A score of 0.51 and a score of 0.95 both fall in "AI territory" but produce different label language — 0.51 gets the uncertain label, 0.95 gets the high-confidence AI label.

False positive asymmetry: I weight the LLM signal slightly more (60%) than stylometrics (40%) because it is more semantically aware. I also bias the uncertain threshold to be wide (0.40–0.64) to reduce false positives — it is worse to wrongly label a human's work as AI than to miss an AI submission.

## Transparency Label Variants

### High-confidence AI (score ≥ 0.65):
> "⚠️ AI-Generated Content Detected — Our analysis suggests this content was likely generated by an AI writing tool (confidence: HIGH). This label is based on automated detection and may not be perfect. If you believe this is incorrect, you can submit an appeal."

### Uncertain (score 0.40–0.64):
> "🔍 Attribution Unclear — Our analysis was unable to determine with confidence whether this content was written by a human or generated by an AI tool (confidence: LOW). We've flagged it for transparency. If you are the human author, you can submit an appeal to provide context."

### High-confidence Human (score < 0.40):
> "✅ Likely Human-Written — Our analysis suggests this content was written by a human (confidence: HIGH). Attribution signals did not detect significant markers of AI-generated text."

## Appeals Workflow

- **Who can appeal:** Any creator who submitted content (identified by creator_id + content_id).
- **What they provide:** `content_id` and `creator_reasoning` (a plain-text explanation).
- **What the system does:** Updates the content's status from "classified" to "under_review" in the audit log. Appends the appeal_reasoning field to that log entry. Returns a confirmation JSON.
- **What a human reviewer sees:** The full audit log entry — original signal scores, confidence, label, timestamp, and the creator's appeal reasoning — everything needed to make a manual decision.

## Anticipated Edge Cases

1. **Formal human academic writing:** A student submitting a well-structured essay with consistent paragraph length and formal vocabulary may score high on stylometrics (low variance = AI-like), even though it is genuinely human. The LLM signal may partially correct this, but it is a known weak spot.
2. **Short poems or haiku:** Texts under ~50 words give the stylometric signal almost no data to work with — sentence length variance and type-token ratio are meaningless at that length. The system will likely produce an "uncertain" label for most short creative writing, which is actually the honest answer.
